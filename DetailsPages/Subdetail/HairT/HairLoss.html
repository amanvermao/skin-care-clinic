Comprehensive Solutions for Hair Loss in Men and Women

Hair loss, whether experienced by men or women, can be a distressing condition that affects self-esteem and quality of life. At The Skin Clinic, we offer comprehensive solutions for various types of hair loss, tailored to address individual needs and concerns.

Understanding Hair Loss:

Hair loss, medically known as alopecia, occurs when there is an interruption in the normal cycle of hair production. This can result in symptoms such as a receding hairline, thinning hair, or bald patches on the scalp. Hair loss can be caused by a variety of factors, including genetic predisposition, nutritional deficiencies, stress, illness, medications, and autoimmune diseases.

Female Pattern Hair Loss and Care:

Female pattern hair loss is characterized by diffuse thinning of hair on the scalp, leading to increased shedding or reduced hair volume. It typically begins with a receding frontal hairline and can progress to a bald patch on the crown. At The Skin Clinic, we offer customized solutions for female pattern hair loss, including topical and oral medications, mesotherapy, PRP therapy, and dietary interventions.

Male Pattern Hair Loss/ Androgenetic Alopecia:

Male pattern hair loss, also known as androgenetic alopecia, is a hereditary condition that affects one in every five men before the age of 30. It typically begins with thinning along the edges and a round bald patch at the crown, eventually leading to extensive baldness. Our treatments for male pattern hair loss include oral medications, topical solutions like minoxidil, PRP therapy, and dermaroller treatments.

Alopecia Areata:

Alopecia areata is an autoimmune condition characterized by partial or complete hair loss on the scalp or other hair-bearing areas of the body. It can manifest as patches of hair loss, total baldness, or diffuse thinning. Treatment options for alopecia areata include intralesional corticosteroid injections, topical minoxidil, and topical corticosteroids, often combined with adjuvant therapies like mesotherapy and PRP.

Telogen Effluvium:

Telogen effluvium is a transient form of hair loss caused by various triggers such as medication, pregnancy, illness, or stress. While there is no specific treatment for telogen effluvium, hair regrowth typically occurs once the underlying cause is addressed. Our specialists can provide guidance and support to manage this condition effectively.

Trichotillomania:

Trichotillomania is a compulsive behavior characterized by the self-induced plucking or breakage of hair, often occurring subconsciously. Treatment may involve psychiatric intervention to address underlying psychological factors contributing to the behavior.

Dandruff:

Dandruff is a common scalp condition characterized by flaking and itching. Treatment options for dandruff include medicated shampoos, topical corticosteroids, and antifungal agents to address underlying causes such as seborrheic dermatitis or psoriasis.

Cicatricial/Scarring Alopecia:

Scarring alopecias result from inflammation and destruction of the hair follicle, leading to irreversible hair loss. Treatment approaches may vary depending on the underlying cause, with options including anti-inflammatory medications, immunomodulators, and surgical interventions.

Conclusion:

Don't let hair loss diminish your confidence and well-being. With our comprehensive range of treatments and personalized care, we can help you regain thicker, healthier hair and restore your confidence.
